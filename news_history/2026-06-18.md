### The Week That Was: A Rollercoaster of Peace, Policy & Pixels! 🎢

**🌍 Geopolitics: From Flashpoints to Formal Signatures!**
The past week was a dizzying ride from near-full-blown conflict to a handshake. It kicked off with **Iran launching missiles at Israel** on Sunday (June 8), followed by swift Israeli retaliation. Yemen's Houthi rebels also piled on with missile attacks and a "complete and total ban" on Israeli shipping in the Red Sea. Tensions hit a peak with a US Apache helicopter being downed near the Strait of Hormuz, prompting **new US strikes against Iran** and Iranian claims of retaliatory strikes on US bases. The UN Secretary-General even warned of a "full war." However, the narrative dramatically flipped mid-week. President Trump canceled planned military strikes, signaling advancing negotiations that culminated in the **US-Iran peace deal announcement** on Monday (June 15). The agreement, confirmed by mediators Pakistan and the EU, aims to end the conflict, includes Iran's commitment not to build nuclear weapons, and, most crucially, plans to **reopen the Strait of Hormuz**. Initial talks between the US and Iran are planned for June 19 in Switzerland to formalize the agreement. Despite the peace, Israeli forces remained in southern Lebanon, with Israeli officials stating Trump's deal "does not bind us," causing friction with Iranian demands. A Russian warship also reportedly fired warning shots at a British yacht in the English Channel.

**💰 Market Movers & Central Bank Whispers: Yields, Hikes & Hawkishness!**
Bond markets have been a geopolitical barometer this week. **US 10-year Treasury yields eased** to 4.44% today, their lowest since May 2026, as cooling inflation fears outweighed the Fed's hawkish tilt. The **Federal Reserve held rates steady** on June 17, but a significant number of officials now foresee a rate hike by year-end, which strengthened the US dollar slightly. Meanwhile, **European bond yields continued to fall** for a fifth consecutive session, with Germany's 10-year Bund hitting its lowest since early April, as the peace deal and lower energy prices suggested moderating inflation in the Eurozone. This follows the **ECB's 25 basis point rate hike to 2.25%** last Thursday (June 11), its first since 2023, driven by energy-fueled inflation. The **Bank of Japan also raised rates to 1%** this week, its highest in 30 years, while the **Bank of England is widely expected to hold steady** today (Thursday).

**🛢️ Oil & Energy: The Hormuz Effect!**
Oil markets experienced whiplash this week. Prices initially surged on conflict escalation, with Brent crude topping $96 a barrel, but then **plummeted over 15%** since the peace deal rumors began swirling. Today, Brent crude is trading around $77.73 a barrel and WTI at $75, nearing pre-war levels as the market anticipates the **reopening of the Strait of Hormuz** and the return of Iranian oil exports. The US will immediately waive sanctions, allowing Iran to sell oil. While OPEC+ had announced a production hike of 188,000 barrels per day from July 2026, its impact is largely dwarfed by the Strait's impending reopening.

**🌾 Agriculture: WASDE, Weather & Worries!**
The **USDA's WASDE report** on June 11 provided clarity: the 2026-2027 U.S. corn outlook remained largely unchanged, and soybean projections were mostly steady. However, **U.S. winter wheat production is forecast to be the lowest since 1965**. Globally, corn and soybean production estimates for South America were raised. Farmers are still grappling with low water levels on the Mississippi River, hiking shipping costs, and rising global fertilizer and diesel prices. Regenerative farming is gaining traction to improve soil health for crops like cotton, corn, soybeans, and wheat. Additionally, cattle farmers are seeing increased beef and steak prices due to decreased supply.

**💰 Metals: Strategic Shifts & Copper Dips!**
Precious metals saw some swings. Gold, after initially reacting to Middle East tensions, surged as the peace deal eased inflation fears and the dollar softened, trading above $4,300 per ounce. Silver and platinum also saw gains. Central banks continue to treat price dips as buying opportunities. Meanwhile, gold mining stocks have entered a bear market despite elevated gold prices, due to higher operating costs and capital allocation choices. In industrial metals, China implemented new regulations on June 15, listing 36 minerals, including rare earths, tungsten, lithium, cobalt, gallium, and germanium, as **national-level strategic minerals**, subjecting them to strict control. This week, several rare earth prices increased for the third consecutive day, and Orient Zirconium raised prices for its zirconium products. Infinity Metals also secured an option to acquire a historic, high-grade Swansea copper mine in Arizona.

**🤖 Tech & AI Frontier: Apple's AI Roadblock & Enterprise Shifts!**
The AI race is still hot! **Apple's WWDC 2026** revealed a completely rebuilt Siri, now largely **powered by Google's Gemini AI models**, aiming to boost iPhone upgrades. However, **EU iPhone and iPad users won't get Siri AI yet** due to concerns surrounding the European Union's Digital Markets Act (DMA), with Apple citing regulatory rejections. Italy's competition regulator even launched an investigation into Apple over iCloud interoperability. Anthropic took its latest models offline to comply with a Trump administration directive restricting foreign use, following a June 2 executive order for *voluntary* AI model cybersecurity tests. Gartner predicts that over 70% of mainframe exit projects will fail due to overestimating generative AI's capabilities. Hewlett Packard Enterprise (HPE) CEO Antonio Neri emphasized AI as a "system-level transformation," highlighting the network as foundational and storage as a bottleneck, while positioning private cloud AI for governed agent deployment. WALLIX and Inria also partnered to develop trusted AI for cybersecurity.

### Broader Implications: A New Era of Global Dynamics 🎲

The **US-Iran peace deal is a monumental game-changer**, abruptly removing a huge geopolitical risk premium from energy markets and fundamentally reshaping inflation expectations. This will have ripple effects across global bond markets, potentially easing the pressure for further aggressive rate hikes, and influencing currency movements as central banks adjust to this new landscape. However, the Federal Reserve's hawkish stance, despite the peace deal, indicates continued vigilance on inflation. The subtle disagreements over the Strait of Hormuz's future toll structure and Israeli troop presence in Lebanon show that while the immediate conflict may be winding down, regional complexities persist. Meanwhile, the AI revolution charges forward, but major tech integrations like Apple's are meeting increased regulatory scrutiny, particularly in the EU, signaling a more controlled and fragmented global approach to AI deployment. This dynamic interplay of newfound peace, ongoing monetary policy adjustments, and the evolving AI frontier promises continued market flux, demanding constant vigilance from traders navigating these interconnected global dramas.
