### The Week That Was: A Rollercoaster of Peace (and Unpeace), Policy & Pixels! 🎢

**🌍 Geopolitics: From Fragile Peace to Renewed Flashpoints!**
The week kicked off with a hopeful bang as the **US and Iran announced an interim peace deal** on Monday (June 15), aiming to end their conflict and reopen the Strait of Hormuz. Formal signing and negotiations were underway in Switzerland, with the US and Iran agreeing to establish a "High-Level Committee" and a "deconfliction cell" to manage tensions, particularly regarding Israel's operations in Lebanon. However, this "peace" proved as stable as a house of cards. Iran swiftly **re-closed the Strait of Hormuz** on June 20-21, accusing Israel of continued attacks in Lebanon and the US of violating the interim deal. President Trump also threatened renewed strikes against Iran if its proxies in Lebanon continued to "cause trouble." These tensions led to Iranian negotiators reportedly walking out of talks in Switzerland on Sunday, though mediators insisted negotiations continued.

**💰 Market Movers & Central Bank Whispers: Fed Hawkishness & Yield Gyrations!**
Bond markets were on a wild ride, reacting intensely to geopolitical shifts and central bank signals. The **Federal Reserve held interest rates steady** last Wednesday (June 17) at 3-1/2 to 3-3/4 percent. However, a hawkish "dot plot" revealed that **nine of 19 policymakers expect a rate hike by year-end** to combat elevated inflation, a move that slightly strengthened the US dollar and boosted Treasury yields. The new Fed Chair, Kevin Warsh, also signaled a shift to less "forward guidance" in communications, which could lead to more market volatility. US 10-year Treasury yields, after initially easing on peace hopes, rose to 4.50% by Monday, driven by inflation concerns and the Fed's stance. Meanwhile, **European bond yields continued to fall** through the week, with Germany's 10-year Bund hitting its lowest since early April, as the initial peace deal and lower energy prices (before the recent Strait re-closure) suggested moderating inflation in the Eurozone. This followed the **ECB's 25 basis point rate hike to 2.25%** last Thursday (June 11), its first since 2023. The **Bank of Japan also raised rates to 1%** this week, its highest in 30 years, while the **Bank of England is widely expected to hold steady**.

**🛢️ Oil & Energy: The Strait Rollercoaster!**
Oil markets experienced whiplash! Prices initially surged past $96 a barrel on conflict escalation early in the week. Then, they plummeted over 15% as the US-Iran peace deal on June 15 promised to reopen the **Strait of Hormuz**, bringing Brent crude below $80. The US had announced immediate waivers on sanctions, allowing Iranian oil to re-enter global markets. However, the narrative flipped again this weekend with Iran's **re-closure of the Strait of Hormuz** on June 20-21, citing renewed Israeli actions in Lebanon. This has pushed Brent crude back above $80. Analysts warn that if disruptions persist, Brent could spike toward $120-$130, with some even floating $150 in a worst-case scenario. The earlier OPEC+ announcement of a production hike of 188,000 barrels per day from July 2026 is now largely overshadowed by the Strait's precarious status.

**🌾 Agriculture: Weather, Trade, and High Costs!**
The **USDA's WASDE report** on June 11 indicated a largely unchanged 2026-2027 U.S. corn outlook and steady soybean projections. However, **U.S. winter wheat production is forecast to be the lowest since 1965**. Globally, corn and soybean production estimates for South America were raised. US farmers continue to grapple with low water levels on the Mississippi River, increasing shipping costs, and rising global fertilizer and diesel prices. Wildfires are also disrupting the summer season, worsening air quality and working conditions for farmworkers. Meanwhile, soybean growers are expected to lose money for the fourth straight year, highlighting the strain of high input costs and trade challenges.

**💰 Metals: Strategic Shifts & Price Swings!**
Precious metals saw significant swings this week. Gold, after initially reacting to Middle East tensions, surged as the peace deal briefly eased inflation fears and the dollar softened, trading above $4,300 per ounce. However, by Monday, gold saw losses, with prices around $4,230 an ounce, and silver and platinum also pulling back following the Fed's hawkish stance. Central banks, including China's, are still treating price dips as buying opportunities. In industrial metals, China has listed 36 minerals, including rare earths, tungsten, lithium, cobalt, gallium, and germanium, as **national-level strategic minerals**, subjecting them to strict control. Rare earth prices for Pr-Nd oxide, dysprosium oxide, and terbium oxide increased for the third consecutive day this week. Copper, critical for AI infrastructure, is expected to see a multi-year upcycle due to underinvestment and physical supply constraints. A bipartisan bill, the **"SILVER Act,"** was introduced to decentralize precious metals storage facilities in the US, aiming to address national security risks from current geographic concentration in New York City.

**🤖 Tech & AI Frontier: Apple's EU Hurdle, Nvidia's Vision, and Enterprise AI Boom!**
The AI race remains intensely competitive. **Apple's WWDC 2026** revealed a completely rebuilt Siri, now largely **powered by Google's Gemini AI models**, allowing users to choose between ChatGPT, Gemini, or Anthropic's Claude. However, **EU iPhone and iPad users won't get Siri AI yet** due to concerns surrounding the European Union's Digital Markets Act (DMA). Italy's regulator is even investigating Apple over iCloud interoperability. Anthropic's latest models, Fable 5 and Mythos 5, remain offline due to a Trump administration directive restricting their use by foreign nationals, a move criticized by over 100 cybersecurity leaders as disproportionate. Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang, speaking at the G7 summit, urged wider AI adoption, stressing its potential for economic growth but also acknowledging public fears about job displacement. He also warned that the US is "woefully behind in energy production," which is critical for supporting energy-hungry AI data centers. In a major enterprise move, **Samsung Electronics is deploying ChatGPT Enterprise and Codex to its global employees**, marking one of OpenAI's largest deployments.

This week has delivered a masterclass in market whiplash, demonstrating the extreme fragility of global stability. The **re-closure of the Strait of Hormuz** is a stark reminder that geopolitical risk premiums, once unwound, can snap back with alarming speed, instantly impacting energy prices and reigniting inflation fears that directly influence central bank decisions on interest rates. This renewed instability will keep bond markets volatile and currency movements unpredictable. Meanwhile, the AI revolution continues its dual trajectory: accelerating integration into major tech ecosystems and enterprises (like Samsung), while simultaneously facing increasing regulatory hurdles, particularly in the EU, and critical infrastructure challenges like energy supply. The interplay of persistent geopolitical tension, cautious central bank policy, and the relentless march of AI ensures a dynamic and challenging environment for global markets.
