### 🚨 Spotlight: Last 24-48 Hours – June 24-25, 2026!

Well, if you thought the Strait of Hormuz saga was dramatic, grab a seat! After Iran's brief closure over the weekend, tanker traffic has largely **returned to normal** through the critical waterway, thanks to a new route near Oman's shores and initial peace deal efforts. US Energy Secretary Chris Wright noted that oil flows are almost back to pre-war levels, with over 20 million barrels moving through in the last 24 hours. However, Iran's Revolutionary Guard is still trying to assert control, warning ships away from unapproved routes and threatening intervention. The US, however, is firmly rejecting any notion of tolls on the Strait.

This easing of supply concerns sent **oil prices tumbling** to near four-month lows! Brent crude is now below $73 a barrel, a level not seen since late February, erasing all war gains. US West Texas Intermediate (WTI) also declined, trading around $69 a barrel.

On the diplomatic front, the US-Iran peace deal is still a mixed bag. While the IAEA head confirmed nuclear site inspections will happen, he indicated "no rush" despite conflicting messages from Washington and Tehran. Plus, Israel's defense minister stated there's "no American demand for Israel to withdraw from Lebanon," directly clashing with Iranian demands for a wider peace deal. US Secretary of State Marco Rubio is busy reassuring Gulf allies that their interests will be protected in these ongoing talks.

In the markets, the **US 10-year Treasury yield rose slightly to 4.41%** today. This comes as the US dollar continued its strengthening trend, reaching its highest level since March 2025, driven by expectations of a more hawkish Federal Reserve. Indeed, traders are increasingly betting on a second US rate hike this year.

Meanwhile, in the AI world, **OpenAI just unveiled its first custom AI chip**, designed in conjunction with Broadcom, aiming to boost its infrastructure and reduce reliance on Nvidia's GPUs.

### The Week That Was: A Geopolitical Soap Opera & Tech Tidal Wave! 🎭

**🌍 Geopolitics: Peace, Then Panic, Then... Partial Peace?**
The week began with a hopeful bang as the **US and Iran announced an interim peace deal** on Monday, June 15th, aimed at ending their conflict and reopening the crucial Strait of Hormuz. Initial talks in Switzerland were set to formalize the agreement. However, this "peace" proved as stable as a house of cards. Iran swiftly **re-closed the Strait of Hormuz on June 20-21**, citing Israeli actions in Lebanon and alleged US deal violations. This caused immediate energy market whiplash. By the end of the week, traffic largely resumed, partly via a new route near Oman, but Iran continued to assert its right to control the Strait and potentially charge tolls – a stance the US firmly rejects. Technical talks in Switzerland concluded, but major disagreements remain over nuclear inspections (despite US claims of "infinity" inspections, Tehran denies it), and the use of unfrozen Iranian assets. Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu is reportedly facing political challenges over the US-Iran deal, as critics say it weakens his ability to influence US policy towards Tehran and potentially reshapes regional dynamics.

**💰 Market Movers & Central Bank Whispers: Hawkish Hues & Yield Swings!**
Bond markets were on a rollercoaster. The **Federal Reserve held interest rates steady** last Wednesday, June 17, but a hawkish "dot plot" revealed that **nine of 19 policymakers expect a rate hike by year-end** to combat elevated inflation. This stance, alongside recent volatility in equity markets, has strengthened the US dollar, which hit a 13-month high. Traders are now assigning a roughly 68% probability of a rate increase in September. US 10-year Treasury yields, after easing early in the week on peace hopes, rose to 4.50% by Monday and are around 4.41% today.
Meanwhile, **European bond yields continued to fall** through much of the week, with Germany's 10-year Bund near three-month lows. This came as the initial peace deal and lower energy prices suggested moderating inflation in the Eurozone, despite the ECB's 25 basis point rate hike last Thursday, June 11, and upward revised inflation forecasts. ECB President Christine Lagarde indicated that the central bank doesn't need to respond more aggressively to Middle East developments, further tempering rate hike concerns. The **Bank of Japan raised rates to 1%** this week, its highest in 30 years, while the **Bank of England is widely expected to hold steady**.

**🛢️ Oil & Energy: The Strait's Shifting Sands!**
Oil markets experienced extreme whiplash. Prices initially surged past $96 a barrel on conflict escalation early in the week, then plummeted over 15% as the US-Iran peace deal promised to reopen the **Strait of Hormuz**, temporarily bringing Brent crude below $80. The narrative flipped again with Iran's brief re-closure, causing prices to rebound. However, with signs of traffic resuming and peace talks continuing (albeit strained), **oil prices are once again sliding**, with Brent crude now below $73 and WTI around $69, erasing all war gains and nearing pre-conflict levels. This sharp drop is attributed to easing fears of supply disruptions and expectations of higher Iranian exports. Analysts warn that if the peace agreement breaks down, a "Strait impasse" remains a constant threat, potentially sending Brent spiking toward $120-$150. Global oil markets are seen entering an era of persistent geopolitical risk and weaponization of energy.

**🌾 Agriculture: Weather Woes & Policy Debates!**
The **USDA's WASDE report** on June 11 indicated a largely unchanged 2026-2027 U.S. corn outlook and steady soybean projections. However, **U.S. winter wheat production is forecast to be the lowest since 1965**. US farmers continue to grapple with low water levels on the Mississippi River, increasing shipping costs, and rising global fertilizer and diesel prices. Wildfires are disrupting the summer season, worsening air quality for farmworkers. Soybean growers are expected to lose money for the fourth straight year. Wheat prices extended their downturn due to rains and harvest pressure, with spring wheat growing areas expecting significant rainfall. Corn prices were slightly lower, and soybean futures saw mixed trading with some recovery in downstream consumption. There's ongoing debate over a **new US Farm Bill (Agricultural Act of 2026)**, with a Republican-led draft proposing support for producers, but critics arguing it increases dependency on petrochemicals. On a brighter note, Kubota invested further in UV Boosting, a French startup using UV light to enhance plant resistance and reduce pesticide use in various crops.

**💰 Metals: Gold's Glitter Fades, Silver Plunges! ✨**
Precious metals had a wild week. Gold initially surged past $4,300 per ounce as the peace deal briefly eased inflation fears and the dollar softened. However, by mid-week, gold prices saw significant losses, falling below $4,000 an ounce for the first time since November 2025, pressured by a stronger US dollar and mounting expectations of Fed rate hikes. Silver outperformed gold on the downside, plunging over 7% in the domestic Chinese market and falling below $62 in international markets, driven by rate hike fears. Platinum also pulled back significantly. Central banks, including China's, continue to treat price dips as buying opportunities. In industrial metals, China implemented new regulations on June 15, listing 36 minerals as **national-level strategic minerals**, including rare earths, tungsten, lithium, cobalt, gallium, and germanium, subjecting them to strict control. Rare earth prices for Pr-Nd oxide, dysprosium oxide, and terbium oxide increased for the third consecutive day this week. Copper is expected to see a multi-year upcycle due to underinvestment and physical supply constraints, crucial for AI infrastructure.

**🤖 Tech & AI Frontier: Custom Chips, Enterprise Rollouts & EU Roadblocks!**
The AI race is intensely competitive. **Apple's WWDC 2026** revealed a completely rebuilt Siri, now largely **powered by Google's Gemini AI models**, allowing users to choose between various AI models for Apple Intelligence features. However, **EU iPhone and iPad users won't get Siri AI yet** due to concerns surrounding the European Union's Digital Markets Act (DMA), with Italy's regulator investigating Apple over iCloud interoperability. Anthropic's latest models remain offline due to a Trump administration directive restricting foreign use. Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang urged wider AI adoption, stressing its economic potential but also acknowledging job displacement fears and warning about the US being "woefully behind in energy production" for AI data centers. In a massive enterprise move, **Samsung Electronics is deploying ChatGPT Enterprise and Codex to its global employees**, marking one of OpenAI's largest deployments. And in a significant infrastructure play, **OpenAI unveiled its first custom AI chip**, designed with Broadcom, to optimize its AI infrastructure and reduce reliance on Nvidia. OpenAI also confidentially submitted a draft S1 to the SEC for a possible public listing. Gartner predicts that over 70% of mainframe exit projects initiated this year will likely fail due to overestimating generative AI's capabilities. A sell-off in big tech companies, particularly AI-driven ones like Micron Technology and Nvidia, occurred earlier in the week due to worries about potentially higher interest rates.

### Broader Implications: Navigating the Whiplash! 🎲

This week has been a masterclass in market whiplash, demonstrating the extreme fragility of global stability. The **US-Iran "peace" deal is proving incredibly tenuous**, with the Strait of Hormuz remaining a critical flashpoint, directly influencing energy markets and, consequently, global inflation concerns. This renewed instability and the Fed's hawkish stance will keep bond markets volatile and currency movements unpredictable, demanding constant vigilance from traders. Meanwhile, the AI revolution charges ahead, but its swift integration is hitting regulatory roadblocks, especially in the EU, and exposing critical infrastructure needs like energy. The interplay of persistent geopolitical tension, cautious central bank policy, and the relentless march of AI ensures a dynamic and challenging environment for global markets, where sudden shifts can create both risk and opportunity.
