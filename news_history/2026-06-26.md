### The Week That Was: A Geopolitical Soap Opera & Tech Tidal Wave! 🎭

**🌍 Geopolitics: From Fragile Peace to Strait-forward Drama!**
The week kicked off with a glimmer of hope as the **US and Iran announced an interim peace deal** on June 15, aiming to end their conflict and reopen the Strait of Hormuz. But that peace proved as fleeting as a Snapchat story. Iran briefly **re-closed the Strait on June 20-21**, citing Israeli actions in Lebanon and alleged US deal violations. While traffic partially resumed, Iran's Revolutionary Guard is still trying to assert control and warn vessels away from "unauthorized routes," a move the US firmly rejects. The vessel attack today just underscores the persistent volatility. Beyond the Strait, Venezuela was rocked by two powerful earthquakes this week, causing widespread damage and fear of thousands of casualties, and a 6.9 magnitude quake also hit northern Japan. Meanwhile, Europe is enduring a record-breaking heatwave, with temperatures soaring and sadly, claiming lives.

**💰 Market Movers & Central Bank Whispers: Hawkish Hues & Yield Swings!**
Bond markets have been on a wild ride, directly mirroring the geopolitical gymnastics. The **Federal Reserve held interest rates steady** last Wednesday (June 17), but their "dot plot" revealed a hawkish tilt, with nine of 19 policymakers expecting a rate hike by year-end to tame inflation. This, along with May's elevated PCE inflation data, has kept US 10-year Treasury yields fluctuating, easing today to 4.38%. The US Dollar Index has seen a strengthening trend, hitting a 13-month high earlier, as traders bet on further Fed hikes. Across the pond, European bond yields generally fell earlier in the week, as initial peace hopes and cooling energy prices suggested moderating inflation, despite the ECB's 25 basis point hike on June 11. The **Bank of Japan raised rates to 1%**, their highest in 30 years, while the Bank of England is expected to hold steady.

**🛢️ Oil & Energy: The Strait's Shifting Sands!**
Oil prices have been a headline act. They surged past $96 on initial conflict escalation, then plummeted over 15% as the US-Iran peace deal promised to reopen the Strait of Hormuz. But then Iran's brief re-closure sent prices rebounding, only for them to slide again as traffic signs resumed (before today's attack). Now, with the vessel attack, prices are back to being highly sensitive. Venezuela's oil production capacity is also under scrutiny after the recent earthquakes.

**🌾 Agriculture: Weather Woes & Policy Debates!**
The **USDA's WASDE report** on June 11 painted a picture of largely unchanged US corn and soybean outlooks, but projected **US winter wheat production to be the lowest since 1965**. Farmers are battling low water levels on the Mississippi River, hiking shipping costs, and rising global fertilizer and diesel prices – all exacerbated by the Strait of Hormuz's volatility. Soybean growers are staring down a fourth consecutive year of losses, while a new US Farm Bill is being hotly debated in the Senate. On a slightly brighter note, Kubota is investing in UV Boosting, a French startup using UV light to reduce pesticide use. President Trump also stated the US will use unfrozen Iranian funds to buy American wheat, soybeans, and corn for Iran.

**💰 Metals: Gold's Glitter Returns, Silver Sees Swings! ✨**
Precious metals have been doing the market cha-cha. Gold initially surged past $4,300 on peace hopes, then dipped below $4,000 as the dollar strengthened and Fed hike expectations grew. However, for June 25, gold futures saw a rebound, up 1.06% to $4030.50, with silver futures up 0.52% to $58.35 and platinum futures up 0.95% to $1587.00. Silver had plunged over 7% earlier due to rate hike fears, while aluminum is heading for its fourth weekly decline due to a stronger dollar and returning Middle East supplies. Central banks are still keen buyers on price dips. China has listed 36 minerals, including rare earths, as **national-level strategic minerals**, and rare earth prices have continued to increase. Copper is expected to see a multi-year upcycle, crucial for AI infrastructure. Guardian Metal's Pilot Mountain Tungsten Project is set to release its Pre-Feasibility Study on June 30.

The AI race is running at full throttle! **Apple's WWDC 2026** revealed a revamped Siri, now largely **powered by Google's Gemini AI models**, giving users a choice of AI. However, **EU iPhone and iPad users won't get Siri AI yet** due to Digital Markets Act concerns. Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang continues to champion AI adoption, while also warning about the US's energy deficit for powering data centers. Samsung is making a huge leap, deploying ChatGPT Enterprise and Codex to its global employees, marking one of OpenAI's largest rollouts. Despite the boom, the massive spending by AI "hyperscalers" like Microsoft, Amazon, and Meta is facing scrutiny, with some of their share prices declining even as chipmakers like Micron Technology report stunning profits.

### Broader Implications: Navigating the Whiplash! 🎲

This week has delivered a masterclass in market whiplash, demonstrating the extreme fragility of global stability. The **renewed tensions and attack in the Strait of Hormuz** serve as a stark reminder that geopolitical risk premiums, once unwound, can snap back with alarming speed, instantly impacting energy prices and reigniting inflation fears. This will keep bond markets volatile and currency movements unpredictable, demanding constant vigilance from traders. Meanwhile, the AI revolution charges forward, but its swift integration is hitting regulatory roadblocks, especially in the EU, and exposing critical infrastructure challenges like energy supply. The global market is playing a high-stakes game of Whac-A-Mole, where geopolitical stability, monetary policy, and transformative technology are all interconnected and prone to sudden shifts, creating both risk and opportunity.
