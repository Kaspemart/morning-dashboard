**The Week That Was: A Rollercoaster of Hope and Havoc!**

**🌍 Geopolitical Rollercoaster & Middle East Meltdown (and now, Mellowing?)**
The week kicked off with a bang (literally!). On Sunday, **Iran launched missiles at Israel**, the first such bombardment since April's fragile ceasefire, and Israel swiftly retaliated. Yemen's Iran-backed Houthi rebels also jumped into the fray, announcing missile attacks on Israel and a "complete and total ban" on Israeli shipping in the Red Sea. Adding to the chaos, a US military base in Saudi Arabia came under fire, and a US Apache attack helicopter was downed near the Strait of Hormuz, prompting **new US strikes against Iran**. Iran, in turn, claimed retaliation on US bases in Kuwait, Bahrain, and Jordan. The UN Secretary-General even warned the risk of a "full war" was rocketing. Amidst all this, a proposed Israel-Lebanon ceasefire was swiftly **rejected by Hezbollah** as an "illusion." However, the narrative shifted dramatically towards week's end as President Trump canceled planned military strikes, signaling advancing negotiations, culminating in Monday's major peace deal announcement.

**💰 Market Movers & Central Bank Whispers! 📈📉**
Bond markets were on a wild ride, directly mirroring the Middle East drama. US Treasury yields eased to 4.44% on June 15, after initially climbing earlier in the week. The 6-month Treasury bill yield also edged down to 3.81% by June 11. European bond yields fell after the ECB hiked its deposit rate by 25 basis points to 2.25%—a move already largely discounted by the market. The US dollar softened as peace hopes emerged, with the EUR briefly rising above 1.1550. This week, we're looking at the Fed's June 16-17 policy meeting, the Bank of Japan potentially raising interest rates to 1%, and the Bank of England likely holding steady.

**🛢️ Oil & Energy: Geopolitical Fuel (Now Damping Down?) ⛽**
Oil prices were the ultimate barometer for Middle East tensions. After soaring on initial conflict escalation, with Brent crude trading above $96 a barrel on Monday, prices plummeted over 4% following the peace deal announcement. Brent dropped below $84 and WTI approached $80 as the **Strait of Hormuz, a critical maritime choke point that had been effectively closed, now stands to reopen**. In a separate, almost forgotten move, the OPEC+ cartel had announced a production hike of 188,000 barrels per day from July 2026, but its impact is clearly overshadowed by the Strait's reopening.

**🌾 Agriculture: Weather Woes & WASDE Watch 🚜**
The USDA's highly anticipated World Agricultural Supply and Demand Estimates (WASDE) report on June 11 provided some clarity. The 2026-2027 U.S. corn outlook remained largely unchanged, while soybean projections were mostly steady. However, the U.S. wheat outlook projected smaller supplies and lower ending stocks, with winter wheat production forecast to be the lowest since 1965. Globally, corn and soybean production estimates for South America were raised. Low water levels on the Mississippi River continue to hike shipping costs for American farmers, and rising global fertilizer and diesel prices are still affecting production costs, adding strain to farmers and consumers.

**💰 Metals: Gold's Glitter & Industrial Shifts ⛏️**
Precious metals saw significant swings this week. Gold, initially impacted by renewed Middle East tensions, surged more than 2% on Monday, trading above $4,300 per ounce, as the US-Iran peace deal eased inflation fears and the dollar softened. Silver outperformed gold, advancing sharply on strong industrial demand and bullion flows, while platinum also saw gains, supported by automotive demand and the hydrogen fuel cell sector. Central banks, including China's, continue to treat price dips as buying opportunities.

**🤖 Tech & AI Frontier: Apple's Gemini Embrace & Regulatory Hurdles! 🚀**
The AI arms race continues with major developments! **Apple's WWDC 2026** revealed a completely rebuilt Siri, now largely **powered by Google's Gemini AI models**! Apple is reportedly shelling out around $1 billion a year for a custom Gemini model to boost iPhone upgrades, allowing users to choose between ChatGPT, Gemini, or Anthropic's Claude for Apple Intelligence features. However, a major snag emerged: **EU iPhone and iPad users won't get Siri AI yet** due to concerns surrounding the European Union's Digital Markets Act (DMA), with Apple citing regulatory rejections of its proposed solutions. In other AI news, Anthropic, an AI giant, took its latest models (Fable 5 and Mythos 5) offline to comply with a Trump administration directive restricting their use by foreign nationals. This follows President Trump's June 2 executive order asking AI developers to *voluntarily* submit their powerful models for government cybersecurity tests. A bipartisan draft bill, the "Great American Artificial Intelligence Act of 2026," also proposed the first comprehensive federal AI governance regime. OpenAI confidentially submitted a draft S1 to the SEC, taking a formal step toward a possible public listing. Earlier in the week, the AI-fueled market rally showed signs of fatigue after Broadcom's earnings miss triggered a broad selloff in semiconductor and technology stocks, raising concerns about AI valuations, but equity markets later rebounded on peace deal hopes and AI spending optimism.
