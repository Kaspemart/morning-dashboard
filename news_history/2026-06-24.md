### The Week That Was: A Geopolitical Soap Opera & Tech Tidal Wave! 🎭

**🌍 Geopolitics: From Shaky Peace to Strait Uncertainty!**
The week was a wild ride from conflict escalation to a fragile peace deal, now shrouded in renewed uncertainty. It kicked off with **Iran launching missiles at Israel** (June 8), followed by swift Israeli retaliation and attacks by Yemen's Houthi rebels. Tensions climaxed with a US Apache helicopter downing near the Strait of Hormuz, leading to new US strikes against Iran. The UN Secretary-General even warned of a "full war." However, the narrative dramatically flipped mid-week as President Trump canceled military strikes, leading to the **US-Iran interim peace deal announcement** on Monday (June 15). This monumental agreement, electronically signed by Presidents Trump and Pezeshkian, aimed to end the conflict, included Iran's commitment not to build nuclear weapons, and, crucially, planned to **reopen the Strait of Hormuz**. Initial talks in Switzerland aimed to formalize the agreement and establish a "High-Level Committee" and "deconfliction cell."

But the "peace" proved fleeting. Iran **re-closed the Strait of Hormuz on June 20-21**, citing Israeli attacks in Lebanon and alleged US violations of the interim deal. This caused immediate energy market whiplash. By today, while there are reports of resumed traffic through a temporary passage opened by Oman and a UN evacuation plan for stranded sailors, Iran continues to assert its sovereign control and right to charge tolls, a stance the US rejects as a violation of international law. Critically, technical talks between the US and Iran in Switzerland have concluded with plans for working groups on sanctions and nuclear issues, but significant disagreements remain, particularly on nuclear inspections and the use of unfrozen Iranian assets. Lebanon's president also stated his country would accept "nothing less" than Israel's withdrawal from southern Lebanon.

**💰 Market Movers & Central Bank Whispers: Hawkish Hues & Yield Swings!**
Bond markets were on a rollercoaster, mirroring geopolitical shifts and central bank signals. The **Federal Reserve held interest rates steady** last Wednesday (June 17) at 3-1/2 to 3-3/4 percent. However, a hawkish "dot plot" revealed that **nine of 19 policymakers expect a rate hike by year-end** to combat elevated inflation. This stance slightly strengthened the US dollar and contributed to Treasury yield fluctuations. US 10-year Treasury yields, after initially easing on peace hopes, rose to 4.50% by Monday, then slightly eased to 4.50% today.

Meanwhile, **European bond yields continued to fall** through the week, with Germany's 10-year Bund hitting its lowest since early April. This decline came as the initial peace deal and lower energy prices (before the recent Strait re-closure drama) suggested moderating inflation in the Eurozone. This followed the **ECB's 25 basis point rate hike to 2.25%** last Thursday (June 11), its first since 2023, driven by energy-fueled inflation. The ECB revised its inflation forecasts upward, projecting headline inflation to average 3.0% in 2026 and remain above 2% through 2027, and signaled a further September hike. The **Bank of Japan also raised rates to 1%** this week, its highest in 30 years, while the **Bank of England is widely expected to hold steady**.

**🛢️ Oil & Energy: The Strait's Shifting Sands!**
Oil markets experienced extreme whiplash this week. Prices initially surged past $96 a barrel on conflict escalation early in the week. Then, they plummeted over 15% as the US-Iran peace deal on June 15 promised to reopen the **Strait of Hormuz**, temporarily bringing Brent crude below $80. The US had announced immediate waivers on sanctions, allowing Iranian oil to re-enter global markets. However, the narrative flipped again with Iran's **re-closure of the Strait of Hormuz** on June 20-21, citing renewed Israeli actions in Lebanon. This caused Brent crude to rebound above $80. But now, with signs of traffic resuming (albeit with ongoing disputes over control and tolls) and peace talks continuing, **oil prices are once again sliding**, with Brent around $76 and WTI near $72. Analysts warn that if disruptions persist, Brent could spike toward $120-$130, with some even floating $150 in a worst-case scenario. The earlier OPEC+ announcement of a production hike of 188,000 barrels per day from July 2026 is largely overshadowed by the Strait's precarious status.

**🌾 Agriculture: Policy Debates & Tech Innovations!**
The **USDA's WASDE report** on June 11 indicated a largely unchanged 2026-2027 U.S. corn outlook and steady soybean projections. However, **U.S. winter wheat production is forecast to be the lowest since 1965**. US farmers continue to grapple with low water levels on the Mississippi River, increasing shipping costs, and rising global fertilizer and diesel prices. Wildfires are also disrupting the summer season, worsening air quality and working conditions for farmworkers. Soybean growers are expected to lose money for the fourth straight year. Meanwhile, a **new US Farm Bill (Agricultural Act of 2026)** is being debated in the Senate, with a Republican-led draft proposing measures to support producers and rural communities, but critics argue it increases dependency on petrochemical pesticides and fertilizers. On a positive note, Kubota announced an additional investment in French startup UV Boosting, which uses UV light to enhance plant resistance to pathogens and reduce pesticide use, aiming to boost yields in crops like wine grapes, fruit trees, and vegetables.

**💰 Metals: Strategic Moves & Copper's Future! ✨**
Precious metals saw significant swings this week. Gold, after initially reacting to Middle East tensions, surged as the peace deal briefly eased inflation fears and the dollar softened, trading above $4,300 per ounce. However, by Monday, gold saw losses, with prices around $4,230 an ounce, and silver and platinum also pulling back following the Fed's hawkish stance. Central banks, including China's, are still treating price dips as buying opportunities. In industrial metals, China has listed 36 minerals, including rare earths, tungsten, lithium, cobalt, gallium, and germanium, as **national-level strategic minerals**, subjecting them to strict control. Rare earth prices for Pr-Nd oxide, dysprosium oxide, and terbium oxide increased for the third consecutive day this week. Copper, critical for AI infrastructure, is expected to see a multi-year upcycle due to underinvestment and physical supply constraints. A bipartisan bill, the **"SILVER Act,"** was introduced to decentralize precious metals storage facilities in the US, aiming to address national security risks.

This week has been a stark reminder that in today's interconnected world, stability is a fleeting mirage. The **US-Iran "peace" deal is proving incredibly tenuous**, with the Strait of Hormuz remaining a significant flashpoint.
