Bond markets have been a wild ride, directly influenced by the Middle East's geopolitical swings. Oil prices are like a sensitive thermometer for Middle East tensions. They tumbled earlier in the week on peace hopes, dipping below $100 a barrel, then surged again on renewed U.S.-Iran hostilities, before today's retreat on the Israel-Lebanon ceasefire news. The **Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed**, continuing to disrupt global energy supplies. Despite the peace optimism, **U.S. crude stockpiles fell by a larger-than-expected 8 million barrels** for the week ended May 29, indicating robust demand and tighter market fundamentals. Exxon and Chevron executives previously warned that global oil stockpiles are nearing historic lows due to the Iran war.

**🌾 Agriculture: Weather Woes & Trade Talk 🚜**

U.S. corn and soybean planting is largely ahead of average nationally, and U.S. sunflower production is estimated to increase by 3% for 2026. However, **low water levels on the Mississippi River are still hiking shipping costs** for American farmers. Rising global fertilizer and diesel prices continue to affect production costs, potentially pushing farmers toward lower-input crops. China has reportedly met an initial commitment to buy 12 million tonnes of U.S. soybeans and agreed to purchase 25 million tonnes annually through 2028, though the market is still looking for concrete proof of sales. Meanwhile, the **U.S. winter wheat harvest is underway**, with 5% cut as of May 31, ahead of the five-year average, but Russia's increased wheat harvest forecast adds to bearish supply. Beyond the screwworm, U.S. rice farmers are having a tough year due to high input costs and low commodity prices.

**💰 Metals: Gold's Glitter & Industrial Shifts ⛏️**

Precious metals have seen swings, with gold experiencing some rebound on safe-haven demand earlier in the week, falling with renewed Middle East tensions, and then seeing gains today on ceasefire hopes. Silver and platinum are showing resilience, partly due to supply challenges and industrial demand.
