Bond markets have been on a wild ride, directly influenced by geopolitical swings and economic data. The 30-year Treasury yield even hit 5.12% this week, its highest since 2007. The ECB warned that financial markets might be underestimating geopolitical and fiscal risks. Eurozone government bond yields also rose, with traders now pricing in a more than 50% probability of three ECB rate hikes by year-end due to energy-driven inflation. The European Central Bank's annual report indicated a shift away from US Treasury bonds towards gold in central bank reserves. The week of June 8-12 is shaping up to be a consequential macro week, with US CPI, the ECB rate decision (a 25bps hike is fully priced), and US PPI all coming before the FOMC meeting.

**🛢️ Oil & Energy: Geopolitical Fuel ⛽**
Oil markets are like a sensitive thermometer for Middle East tensions. Prices tumbled on peace hopes earlier in the week, dipped below $100 a barrel, then surged on renewed US-Iran hostilities, briefly retreated on the short-lived Israel-Lebanon ceasefire news, before rising again sharply today with the Hezbollah rejection and an explosion at Oman's Mina al Fahal oil terminal, allegedly from a drone attack. The Strait of Hormuz's effective closure continues to disrupt global oil and gas supplies. Exxon and Chevron executives previously warned that global oil stockpiles are nearing historic lows due to the Iran war. The US Strategic Petroleum Reserve (SPR) is being drained at a record pace. US crude exports hit a record high in May as refiners sought alternatives to Middle Eastern supply. Despite ongoing supply issues, the OPEC+ cartel plans to hike oil production by 188,000 barrels a day from July.

US corn and soybean planting is largely ahead of average, and sunflower production is estimated to increase by 3% for 2026. However, winter wheat conditions remain a concern, with 44% rated poor to very poor. Low water levels on the Mississippi River are still hiking shipping costs for American farmers. Rising global fertilizer and diesel prices continue to affect production costs, with diesel seeing a "biggest shock since 2022." China reportedly met an initial commitment to buy 12 million tonnes of US soybeans and agreed to purchase 25 million tonnes annually through 2028. Soybean, corn, and wheat futures were lower midweek due to favorable US crop weather boosting yield prospects and ample global supplies. The **New World screwworm was detected in a Texas calf**, the first US appearance since 1966, prompting swift containment actions. The USDA's highly anticipated June WASDE report on June 11 could trigger major price swings. Agricultural production is also threatened by a combination of El Niño conditions and high input prices globally.
